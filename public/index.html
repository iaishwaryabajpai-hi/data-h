🔬 The Paradox Explained

Women own homes at the same rate as men — but earn 20% less and own cars at 2.5× lower rates. This reveals that realty ownership is often inherited or shared, while income and vehicle ownership reflect individual economic agency. Credit scoring that weights realty ownership equally may mask deep financial vulnerability.

The 5-Why Drill

1 Why do women earn less? → Concentrated in lower-paying occupations
2 Why those occupations? → Education pipeline (69% secondary-only)
3 Why less education access? → Family responsibilities (higher child-care burden)
4 Why isn't realty wealth offsetting? → Realty is household-level, income is individual
5 Why does this matter for credit? → Risk models using household assets overestimate women's individual creditworthiness

09 — RECOMMENDATIONS

Policy Recommendations

01

Gender-Aware Credit Scoring

Current models may systematically disadvantage women by weighting income heavily. Incorporate income-to-asset ratio instead of raw income to level the playing field.

02

Occupation Data Mandate

30% missing occupation data creates blind spots. Mandate occupation field collection — the missing data correlates with lower income brackets, biasing risk models.

03

Age-Adjusted Products

The Dual-Cohort pattern (peaks at 30s and 50s) suggests two distinct product lines: growth-oriented for young professionals and stability-focused for pre-retirees.

04

Education-Income Pipeline

The 34% education return gap between genders suggests systemic barriers. Financial products should account for income trajectory potential, not just current earnings.
